My Unofficial Guide to Life


By Lily Dunham

Control

It is so easy to feel that you have no control over your life. In an envirnment where so much is required and routines are locked it can feel impossible to make choices for yourself. Too often, people lose touch of the part of themselves full of initiative and drive. Its often easier to exist than to live. The trick to feeling as if you are happening to life just as much as it is happeing to you is simple. You have to make more choices. Choices give you control. When you wake up, be intentional with your outfit. Really think about what you want to wear. Try a new food for breakfast. Walk to school or try a different route than you normally take. Come home and bake a cake. We make so many choices each day but once we settle into routines, those choices are made for us. When you say no to yourself more than you say yes, you lose control. We live in a world with an abundance of freedom that we don't even realize we have. There is so much more that you can do then you can't do and if you don't let yourself try, you'll never know. Spontineity is your superpower. Once you start to live with the awareness of possibility then you no longer surrender your control to routines and to other people.



Social Philosophy

People are complex beings. We love to judge and then hate to be judged. We love to talk about ourselves until we're being talked about. The ideal of not caring what people think about you is rarely applicable to someones real life. What you can do is choose to weigh each opinion logically. Take away the pressure of agreeing with all or nothing that someone else believes about you. You can choose to agree with what celebrates your being or helps you grow, and nothing else matters. If you spend more time thinking about what other people think about you than you do considering what you think about them, this is your sign to make a change. A different version of you exists in the minds of everyone you know, and none of them are you completely. Once you learn to accept that you're free. Essetially, who you are is for you to create. Sartrarian philosophy tells us that we are our choices. If you don't like who you are, become the person you want to be simply by living with the choices that they would make. If you want to change yourself, do. It's not such a terrible thing as we're led to believe; however we must live for ourselves, and therefore if you aren't changing for yourself, but rather for other people, it's not worth changing at all.



Motivation

Once you realize that you are the only person who can truly control what you do, you can finally start getting things done. You are the only person who can harness the wind in your sails and get your boat moving. If you can convince yourself that the work you are doing is worth doing then you will have endless motivation. If you can't bring yourself to start your homework, no logic from a teacher or parent will bring you to do it, you need to find ways to want to do it. Eat an oreo every time you finish an assignment. Work in a place you find beautiful. It has to come from you. Pick the rewards that will make you happy. Set up that system for yourself, and then learn to hold yourself accountable. This is how you create authentic and interestign work. If you aren't pushing yourself to be great, nobody else will be able to get you there.
